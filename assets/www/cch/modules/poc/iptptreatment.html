Intermittent Preventive Treatment (IPTp) with Sulfadoxine-Pyrimethamine (SP) for Malaria Prevention in Pregnant Women



Target groups for IPTp using SP include:
  • All pregnant women with no signs clinical of malaria whose pregnancies are between 16-36 weeks of gestation

  • Women with first or second pregnancies

  • Adolescent and young pregnant women (10-24 years)

  • Pregnant women with sickle cell disease

  • Pregnant women with HIV infection and other immunocompromised states


Pregnant women with the following conditions should be exempted from using SP:

  • Women in their 1st trimester of pregnancy (first 3 months)

  • Women known to have Glucose-6-Phosphate Dehydrogenase (G6PD) enzyme deficiency, either full or partial defect

  • Women known to have severe liver disease

  • Women with unexplained recurrent jaundice

  • Women who are known to be allergic to sulphur-containing drugs or pyrimethamine component

  • Women who have received treatment with sulphur-based drugs, e.g. cotrimoxazole within 4 weeks of being seen at the clinic

  • Women with HIV/AIDS who have been put on cotrimoxazole for opportunistic infections

  • Women with pregnancy beyond 36 weeks (8 months) gestation

  • Women breastfeeding
Dosage of SP for IPTp:

New GHS guidelines indicate that pregnant women should take up to 5 doses of SP for antimalarial prophylaxis.

A single dose compromising 3 tablets of 500 mg sulfadoxine + 25 mg pyrimethamine taken with clean water.

Ensure pregnant clients eat BEFORE taking the drug.
The following is the dosage schedule for SP:

  • 1st dose at 16 wks gestation The 1st dose should be administered by a midwife, therefore refer clients to the health facility.

  • 2nd dose at least 4 weeks after 1st dose

  • 3rd dose at least 4 weeks after 2nd dose

  • 4th dose at least 4 weeks after 3rd dose

  • 5th dose at least 4 weeks after 4th dose

Note: The last dose of SP can be administered up to the time of delivery without any safety concerns. Source: WHO
Counselling/Education on Use of SP
  • All pregnant women should be educated and counselled on IPTp using SP on an individual basis prior to drug administration

  • Women who receive SP should be educated on the potential adverse effects and what to do when these arise

  • Women who refuse IPTp using SP for any reason should NOT be discriminated against, and should be counselled to use ITN
