Rest and Exercise

Here are some exercises you can encourage your clients to do:

Pelvic Rock

Helps relieve backache and pressure in abdomen, and strengthens muscles in the abdomen

  1. Get down on your hands and knees, as show in the top figure.

  2. Pull in your abdomen and lif your buttocks, as shown in the bottom figure. Hold for a count of 5.

  3. Gently rock your abdomen and buttocks, allowing the curve of your back to return.

  4. Repeat 5 times.

Head and Shoulder lift

Strengthens muscles in the abdomen

  1. Lie on your back with your knees bent, feet on the floor, and your arms at sides as shown.

  2. Raise your head and shoulders and tighten your abdominal muscles. Hold for a count of 5. Do not hold your breath.

  3. Lie back and relax.

  4. Repeat 5 to 10 times.

Squat

Strengthens leg muscles



  1. Stand while holding on to something to help you to keep your balance. Keep your feet apart.

  2. Slowly bend your knees keeping your back straight and keeping your knees and feet apart.

  3. Rise slowly, keeping hold of the object for balance.

  4. Repeat 3 to 5 times.

Rib Cage Lift

Strengthens leg muscles and makes it easier to breathe



  1. Sit with your legs crossed as shown

  2. Curve your arm over your head, and then return to the original position.

  3. Repeat 4 to 5 times, then do the same with the other arm.

Kegel Exercise

Strengthens muscles that help make delivery easier, and reduces the chance of complications during delivery


  1. Tighten the muscles around your anal opening in your vagina (as if you were holdnig back urine). Hold for a count of three, then relax.

  2. Repeat 5 to 10 times. The exercise can be done any time during the day when you are sitting down.
